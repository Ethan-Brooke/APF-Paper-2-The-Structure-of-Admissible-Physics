# Do Not Claim — Paper 2

1. **Do not claim Theorem R rules out all unified theories.** It rules out admissibility frameworks that close on a **single** irreducible representation. GUTs with multiple representations are not excluded by Theorem R alone.

2. **Do not claim Paper 2 proves $L_{\rm gauge\_template\_uniqueness}$.** The proof is in Paper 1 Supp §5; Paper 2 uses it.

3. **Do not claim Paper 2's $L_{\rm count} = 61$ is an independent derivation.** It counts the irreps that Theorem R + Paper 1's template force. The count is structural arithmetic on forced inputs.

4. **Do not claim Paper 2 derives Hilbert space structure.** That is Paper 5.

5. **Do not claim Paper 2 derives $\Omega_\Lambda$.** The I2 statement at the $L_{\rm count}$ seam establishes that $K = 61$ is the shared integer — full bridge content (uniqueness of $V_\Lambda$) is Paper 8 Theorem 1.1.

6. **Do not claim $T_M$ (monogamy) is a conservation law.** It is a structural monogamy of admissibility interfaces, not a Noether-type conservation.

7. **Do not claim the 1-of-4680 filter is in Paper 2.** $T_{\rm field}$ is defined in Paper 1; the 4680-candidate enumeration and filtering is carried out in Paper 4.

8. **Do not claim Paper 2 resolves the hierarchy problem.** It forces the gauge structure; mass hierarchies are Paper 4.

9. **Do not claim 61 is a free parameter.** It is the arithmetic sum of forced counts.

10. **Do not claim Theorem R is a no-go theorem for all unification.** It applies only inside the admissibility framework and for single-representation closure.
